Hello. My name is Adam Rule and I am a PhD student in Cognitve Science at the University of California, San Diego. I study human-computer interaction by developing technologies for complex and fragmented work. I have particular interests in reducing the impact of interruptions, exploring the potential of multi-modal interfaces, and supporting the use of information technology in developing countries, a field known as ICTD.

My past work has spanned the design - engineering divide. I've optimized hospital scheduling to reduce patient wait time, designed respect into an assistive robot, and made health software more usable.
