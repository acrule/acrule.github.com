Hello. My name is Adam Rule. I am a Masters student in Human Centered Design & Engineering at the University of Washington. I study human-computer interaction in complex and safety-critical fields such as healthcare and robotics. My current interests lie at the intersection of health, cognition, and information technology. I am particularly interested in how clinicians use health information technologies while under high levels of interruption and stress. I am also interested in how information technology can address issues of health, employment, and education in developing countries, a field known as ICTD.


I have a background in industrial engineering, human-computer interaction, and design. I've modified hospital scheduling policies to reduce patient wait time, designed the interaction of an assistive robot to be respectful, and improved the usability of health equipment management software used in several African countries. I've also done user research with Amazon.
