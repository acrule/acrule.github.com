Hi, I'm Adam Rule

I am a postdoctoral scholar with Michelle Hribar and Michael Chiang in the Casey Eye Institute at Oregon Health and Science University where I study the how providers use Electronic Health Records to mix text and data in clinical notes. I received my PhD in Cognitive Science from UC San Diego where I worked with Jim Hollan in the Design Lab to study how academic researchers use Jupyter Notebooks to analyze data and share insights.

I study and develop technologies that help people document, share, and collaboratively perform data-driven work. I get particularly excited when talking about computational notebooks, electronic health records, reproducible research, information visualization, and apple fritters.

I am on the job market for faculty positions in human-computer interaction.
