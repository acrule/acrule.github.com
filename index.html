Adam Rule

CV · adam.rule@wisc.edu

Adam Rule is an Assistant Professor in the Information School at the University of Wisconsin-Madison, where he is part of the Collaborative Computing Group.

Adam studies how people use interactive documents such as clinical notes and computational notebooks to mix data and narrative text. Adam received a PhD in Cognitive Science from UC San Diego—where he worked with Jim Hollan in the Design Lab— and completed a post-doctoral fellowship in Medical Informatics at Oregon Health & Science University. He has been generously supported by the National Library of Medicine through their Informatics Training Program.
