Hello. My name is Adam Rule and I am a PhD student in Cognitve Science at the UC San Diego. I study human-computer interaction, developing technologies for complex and fragmented work. I have particular interests in interruptions, information visualization, and clinical informatics.

In my past work, I've optimized hospital scheduling to reduce patient wait time, designed interactions with an assistive robot, and improved the usability of health information technology.

I'm currently studying how writers, programmers, and artists get back into the flow of working after taking a day or two off. If you are currently working on a long-term writing, programing, or artisitic project, use a Mac, and are part of the UC San Diego community, you can participate! Participants get $50 in gift cards.

You can reach me at acrule@ucsd.edu.
