Current Research

Interruption Recovery

I'm working with Jim Hollan and Aurelien Tabard on software to help mitigate the impact of interruptions. Interruptions - by colleagues, computer alerts, and even self redirection - are a staple of knowledge work. They keep us up to date with relevant information and help us prioritize work. However, they can also make us forget what we were doing before being redirected. Visual stimuli have been shown to evoke rich episodic memory about past events, bringing to mind not only what was happening, but also why and how it was occuring. Well-designed information visualizations of past computer activity could help aleviate the cost of interruption.

ActiveNotes

Clinicians often have to document actions that were taken hours ago. For example, orders for medications, labs, or imaging are often placed during rounds, but documented in the note hours later. At this point the full rationale for the order may be hard to remember. I'm working with the Veterans' Medical Research Foundation on a new interface for note creation that lets semi-structured text entery be used to document and place orders at the same time.

Past Projects

Robot Teams

I conducted research with Dr. Jodi Forlizzi on the information needed to maintain situation awareness in multi-user, multi-robot systems. A paper on this work was published at HRI 2012.

Home Exploring Robotic Butler

I conducted research with Dr. Jodi Forlizzi on the interaction design of the Home Exploring Robotic Butler. I conducted an ethnographic case study of the relationship between a young man with quadriplegia and his caregiver and used this study to inform the robot's design.
