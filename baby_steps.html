Baby Steps

How can technology help parents track their child's development?

Background

Dates: September 2011 - December 2011

Role: Team Member

The state of Washington is interested in creating a central database for tracking child development. Such a system could open channels between public schools, parents, and health care providers and aid in identifying developmental delays. Professor Julie Kientz at the University of Washington is studying how novel technology can be used to track child development. Of particular interest is how traditionally under-served populations may access and submit child development information. I worked with Professor Kientz and several other students at the University of Washington to study how technology may be used by the Latino population to track child development.

Process

  • Semi-Structured Interviews - Our team interviewed several health advocates and healthcare providers for the Latino population in the Seattle area. Interviews of parents are still underway.

  • Future: Prototyping
