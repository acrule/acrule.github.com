Baby Steps

How can technology help parents track their child's development?


Background

Dates: September - December 2011
Advisor: Prof. Julie Kientz

The state of Washington is interested in creating a central database for tracking child development. Such a system could open channels of communication between public schools, parents, and health care providers and aid in identifying developmental delays. Professor Julie Kientz at the University of Washington is studying how novel technology can be used to track child development. Of particular interest is how traditionally under-served populations may access and submit child development information. I worked with Professor Kientz and several other students at the University of Washington to study how technology may be used by the Latino population to track child development.

Process
